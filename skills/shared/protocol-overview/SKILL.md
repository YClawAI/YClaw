## What YClaw Is

YClaw is an attention rewards protocol on Solana. Watchers boost creator leaderboard scores by watching content, creators launch personal tokens with bonding curve pricing, and stakers earn options by staking creator tokens. The monthly Creator Rewards Contest connects watching to on-chain rewards — leaderboard position determines what percentage of accrued options get minted each period.

YClaw is not a social network. It is not a content platform. It is not a financial product. It is a protocol that rewards the act of watching.

---

## Three Participant Types

YClaw has three types of participants. A person can be all three simultaneously.

### 1. Watchers

Watchers contribute to the Creator Rewards Program by watching content through the YClaw Chrome extension. The extension runs silently in the background, detecting when the user is on a supported platform and tracking verified watch time.

- Install the Chrome extension and connect a Solana wallet
- Watch content on any supported platform (no cost, no staking required)
- Watch time is tracked and weighted by viewer type (stakers get 9x weight)
- Monthly leaderboard ranks creators by their viewers' weighted watch time
- Creator's leaderboard position determines what percentage of their accrued options get minted

Watching is free. No SOL, no tokens, no upfront cost. But watching while staking gives the creator a 9x leaderboard boost per minute.

### 2. Creators

Creators launch personal tokens on YClaw with bonding curve pricing. Their audience can buy, hold, and stake these tokens.

- Permissionless — anyone can launch a token
- Each creator gets a unique SPL token tied to their bonding curve
- Creators earn a configurable share of rewards generated by their watchers and stakers
- The creator/staker rewards split is set per market via `creator_rewards_share_percentage` (0-100%)

### 3. Stakers

Stakers buy creator tokens on the bonding curve, then stake (deposit) those tokens into the market to earn options.

- Options accrue continuously based on protocol parameters (rate is DAO-controlled)
- Options are minted and exercised into real tokens when a period closes (held by the program until claimed)
- The creator's leaderboard position determines what percentage of options get minted each period
- Stakers who also watch contribute a 9x multiplier to the creator's leaderboard score

## How Creator Tokens Work

### Bonding Curve

Each creator launches a personal token (e.g., `$KRAB` for a creator named MrKrabs). The token uses bonding curve pricing — a mathematical formula, not an order book.

**Properties:**
- **Linear curve:** Price rises predictably as more tokens are bought, falls as they are sold
- **Price floor:** Minimum price the token can reach
- **Deterministic:** Given a supply, the price is calculable
- **Continuous:** No cliffs, tiers, or discrete pricing levels
- **On-chain:** The curve logic lives in a Solana program (Mayflower AMM)

**Buying and selling:**
- **Buy:** Send USDC to the bonding curve, receive creator tokens
- **Sell:** Send creator tokens to the bonding curve, receive USDC
- Both have slippage protection (minimum output amount)
- Both have fees (configurable per market group)

Early buyers get lower prices. The price rises as more people buy.

## How the Creator Rewards Program Works

The Creator Rewards Program is the core loop of YClaw. It connects watching (off-chain) to staking rewards (on-chain) through a monthly leaderboard contest.

### The Full Loop

```
1. Stakers stake creator tokens → options accrue per-second on the market account
2. Watchers watch creators → leaderboard scores accumulate based on weighted watch time
3. Monthly period close: creator's leaderboard position determines mint percentage (4-100%)
4. Options minted and exercised into real creator tokens (held by the program, not users)
5. Tokens split between creator (their share %) and stakers (remainder)
6. Creator and stakers claim their tokens from the market
7. Unminted options roll over to the next period
```

### Leaderboard Score

Each creator's score is calculated from viewer watch time, weighted by viewer type:

| Viewer Type | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| Anonymous | 0x (excluded) |
| Signed-in user | 1x |
| Follower | 1.25x |
| Subscriber | 6x |
| Staker | 9x |

Stakers who also watch contribute 9x to a creator's score — the highest multiplier.

### Leaderboard Position → Mint Percentage

At period close, leaderboard position determines what percentage of accrued options get minted:

- 1st place: 100%
- 2nd place: 96%
- Decreases by 4% per position
- 25th place: 4%
- Below 25th: 0% (all options roll over)

**Unminted options are not lost.** They carry forward to the next period.

### Options Are Held by the Program

Options tokens are never sent directly to user wallets. The flow is:

1. Options accrue as an abstract counter (`exercisable_options`) on the market account
2. At period close, options are minted to the market's token account (a PDA)
3. Options are immediately exercised into real creator tokens in the same account
4. Users claim their share of these real tokens via claim instructions

### Creator/Staker Split

- Each market has a `creator_rewards_share_percentage` (0-100, default 70%)
- Creator gets that percentage of minted tokens, stakers get the rest
- **Decreases take effect immediately** (benefits stakers)
- **Increases take effect at the start of the next month** (prevents abuse)

### Options Accrual

- Rate set by `yearly_options_accrual_bps` (DAO-controlled, market group level)
- Accrues per-second per-staked-token, continuously
- `update_index()` runs on every deposit, withdrawal, trade, period close, or split change

### Period Close

- Periods are closed by an admin (monthly cadence, not time-locked on-chain)
- The admin provides the `mint_percentage_options` based on leaderboard position
- On-chain constraint: mint percentage must be 4-100%
- Minted amount = `exercisable_options × mint_percentage / 100`
- Remainder stays in `exercisable_options` for the next period

### Claiming

- After period close, stakers call `accrue_position_rewards` to convert their share to claimable
- Then call `claim_staker_rewards` to transfer tokens to their wallet
- Creators call `claim_creator_rewards` directly
- `claimable = earned_rewards - claimed_rewards`

## The Watch Protocol (Chrome Extension)

### How Watching Works

1. Extension detects the user is on a supported platform
2. Content script identifies the creator/channel
3. Background service creates a viewer session via API
4. Heartbeats sent periodically while watching
5. Watch time is recorded and weighted by viewer type (signed-in, follower, subscriber, staker)
6. Weighted watch time feeds the monthly Creator Rewards leaderboard
7. Creator's leaderboard position determines their mint percentage at period close

### Supported Platforms

YouTube, Twitch, Kick, Rumble, X, Parti, Arena, Abstract, Pump.fun — nine platforms at launch.

### Extension States

| State | What it shows |
|---|---|
| Watching | Green dot, creator name, platform, session timer, leaderboard contribution |
| Not watching | "Not tracking" message |
| Creator on YClaw | Creator profile link, token info |
| Creator not on YClaw | "This creator isn't on YClaw yet" |
| Not logged in | Login/connect wallet prompt |

## Leaderboard

- Monthly leaderboard based on weighted watch time (the Creator Rewards Contest)
- Creator score = sum of (viewer_minutes × viewer_multiplier) across all their viewers
- Viewer multipliers: Anonymous 0x, Signed-in 1x, Follower 1.25x, Subscriber 6x, Staker 9x
- Top 25 creators earn mint percentages (1st=100%, 25th=4%) that determine their reward payout
- Categories: Global + per-content-category
- Leaderboard position directly determines how much of a creator's accrued options get realized each period

- A percentage fee is applied on each buy transaction
- A percentage fee is applied on each sell transaction
- Fees are split between the protocol treasury and the creator
- Fee rates are defined in the curve state and visible on-chain

## What YClaw Is Not

- **A financial product.** Staking provides rewards from protocol activity, not guaranteed financial returns.
- **An exchange.** Creator tokens are not traded against each other. Each has its own bonding curve.
- **A social network.** YClaw does not host content, messages, or social feeds. It wraps around existing platforms.
- **An investment platform.** No APY promises, no guaranteed returns.
- **A prediction market.** Bonding curves reflect demand for creator tokens, not price speculation.
- **A platform.** YClaw is a protocol. It wraps around platforms — it does not replace them.

These distinctions matter for legal compliance, brand voice, and community expectations. When in doubt, frame YClaw as "an attention rewards protocol" — nothing more, nothing less.
